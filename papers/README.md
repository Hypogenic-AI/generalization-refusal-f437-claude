# Downloaded Papers

## Core Papers (directly relevant to hypothesis)

1. **Emergent Misalignment: Narrow finetuning can produce broadly misaligned LLMs** (2502.17424_emergent_misalignment.pdf)
   - Authors: Betley, Tan, Warncke, Sztyber-Betley, Bao, Soto, Labenz, Evans
   - Year: 2025 (Nature Jan 2026)
   - arXiv: 2502.17424
   - Why relevant: Mirror image of our hypothesis — narrow misalignment generalizes broadly. Provides methodology template.

2. **Refusal in Language Models Is Mediated by a Single Direction** (2406.11717_refusal_single_direction.pdf)
   - Authors: Arditi, Obeso, Syed, Paleka, Panickssery, Gurnee, Nanda
   - Year: 2024 (NeurIPS 2024)
   - arXiv: 2406.11717
   - Why relevant: Mechanistic explanation — refusal is a single direction, so amplifying it via fine-tuning should generalize.

3. **Fine-tuning Aligned Language Models Compromises Safety, Even When Users Do Not Intend To!** (2310.03693_finetuning_compromises_safety.pdf)
   - Authors: Qi, Zeng, Xie, Chen, Jia, Mittal, Henderson
   - Year: 2023 (ICLR 2024)
   - arXiv: 2310.03693
   - Why relevant: Shows benign fine-tuning erodes safety (reduces refusal). Our hypothesis is the reverse direction.

## Evaluation & Benchmarking Papers

4. **OR-Bench: An Over-Refusal Benchmark for Large Language Models** (2405.20947_or_bench.pdf)
   - Authors: Cui, Chiang, Stoica, Hsieh
   - Year: 2024 (ICML 2025)
   - arXiv: 2405.20947
   - Why relevant: Provides over-refusal benchmark and shows safety-refusal correlation (0.878 Spearman).

5. **XSTest: A Test Suite for Identifying Exaggerated Safety Behaviours** (2308.01263_xstest_exaggerated_safety.pdf)
   - Authors: Röttger et al.
   - Year: 2023 (NAACL 2024)
   - arXiv: 2308.01263
   - Why relevant: 250 safe + 200 unsafe prompts for measuring exaggerated safety.

## Related Mechanism & Attack Papers
